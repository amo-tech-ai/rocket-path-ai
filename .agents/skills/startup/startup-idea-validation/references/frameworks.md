# Analysis Frameworks

## Market Opportunity Assessment

### TAM/SAM/SOM
- **TAM:** Total market demand for product/service
- **SAM:** Segment of TAM targeted by your products
- **SOM:** Portion of SAM you can realistically capture (3-5 year)

### Market Indicators
- Number of potential customers
- Average revenue per customer
- Market growth rate (CAGR)
- Geographic distribution
- Industry spending trends

## Competitive Landscape

### Porter's Five Forces
1. **Threat of New Entrants:** Barriers, capital requirements
2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers:** Concentration, switching costs
3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers:** Customer concentration, price sensitivity
4. **Threat of Substitutes:** Alternative solutions, switching costs
5. **Competitive Rivalry:** Number of competitors, market growth

### Competitor Categories
- **Direct:** Same solution, same market
- **Indirect:** Different solution, same problem
- **Adjacent:** Similar solution, different market
- **Emerging:** New technologies or business models

## Problem-Solution Fit

### Problem Validation Criteria
- **Frequency:** How often does the problem occur?
- **Intensity:** How painful is it?
- **Willingness to Pay:** Are customers paying for solutions now?
- **Accessibility:** Can you reach affected customers?

### Solution Evaluation
- Unique Value Proposition
- Competitive Advantage (sustainable?)
- Technology Moat
- Network Effects

## Risk Categories

- **Market:** Size too small, wrong timing
- **Execution:** Team gaps, operational challenges
- **Technology:** Feasibility, scalability
- **Competitive:** Incumbents, better-funded rivals
- **Regulatory:** Compliance, licensing
- **Financial:** Funding requirements, burn rate

## Positive Signals
- Growing market with favorable trends
- Multiple customer segments with shared problem
- Existing budget/spending for similar solutions
- Low customer acquisition costs
- Strong word-of-mouth potential

## Warning Signs
- Shrinking or stagnant market
- Heavy reliance on single segment
- High CAC or long sales cycles (>6 months B2B)
- Strong incumbents with high switching costs
- Unclear monetization path

# Porter's Five Forces: Strategic Market Analysis Guide

**Source:** [Foundor.ai Blog – Porter's Five Forces](https://foundor.ai/en/blog/porters-five-forces)  
**Topic:** Industry dynamics, competitive landscape analysis

---

## Summary

Porter's Five Forces (Michael E. Porter, 1979) evaluates five competitive forces that shape industry profitability. The framework helps entrepreneurs and managers assess market attractiveness, identify competitive advantages, detect risks early, and support strategic decisions. Especially valuable for startups to avoid unrealistic market assessments.

---

## The Five Core Elements

| Force | What it analyzes |
|-------|------------------|
| **1. Threat of New Entrants** | Ease of new competitors entering; barriers: capital, regulation, distribution, economies of scale, brand loyalty |
| **2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers** | Extent suppliers can dictate prices; factors: number of suppliers, uniqueness, switching costs, forward integration |
| **3. Bargaining Power of Buyers** | Customer influence on prices/terms; factors: concentration, alternatives, price sensitivity, buyer information |
| **4. Threat of Substitute Products** | Extent alternatives could replace your offering; criteria: availability, price-performance, switching costs, tech developments |
| **5. Competitive Rivalry** | Direct competition among existing firms; factors: number/size of competitors, growth, differentiation, exit barriers, fixed costs |

## Step-by-Step Guide

### 1. Define and delimit the industry

Precisely define the industry for meaningful analysis.  
*Example:* "Online clothing subscription services with a focus on accessories"

### 2. Data collection and research

Gather: market size and growth, main competitors and shares, supplier structure, customer behavior, regulatory framework.

### 3. Evaluate each force (rating 1–5)

| Force | Rating (1–5) |
|-------|--------------|
| Threat of new entrants | ___/5 |
| Bargaining power of suppliers | ___/5 |
| Bargaining power of buyers | ___/5 |
| Threat of substitutes | ___/5 |
| Competitive rivalry | ___/5 |

### 4. Analysis and interpretation

Identify strongest forces, opportunities, risks, strategic recommendations.

### 5. Strategy development

Develop concrete positioning and differentiation strategies.

## Practical Example: Sock Subscription Service

| Force | Rating | Reasoning |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **New Entrants** | 4/5 | Low barriers (e-commerce, dropshipping); protective: loyal base, unique designs, logistics complexity |
| **Supplier Power** | 2/5 | Many manufacturers globally; standardized production; can switch suppliers |
| **Buyer Power** | 3/5 | Easy price comparison, low switching costs, many alternatives; offset by personalization, convenience |
| **Substitutes** | 4/5 | Traditional retail, other accessory subs, one-time purchases, custom services |
| **Rivalry** | 4/5 | Many sock subscription services; aggressive pricing; low differentiation |

**Overall:** 3.4/5 = Medium–high competitive intensity → strategic differentiation essential.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Too narrow industry definition | Think in customer needs, not just product categories |
| Static view | Conduct regularly; monitor trends |
| Subjective evaluation without data | Collect quantitative data; use multiple sources |
| Isolation from other tools | Combine with SWOT, customer analysis, market trends |
| Neglecting force interactions | Examine how forces influence each other |

---

## Strategic Recommendations

| Force | Actions |
|-------|---------|
| **High new entrants** | Build brand, loyalty programs, economies of scale, exclusive supplier contracts |
| **Supplier power** | Diversify suppliers, long-term partnerships, vertical integration, alternative materials |
| **Buyer power** | Add value through service, customer segmentation, lock-in via personalization, price differentiation |
| **Substitutes** | Unique value prop, continuous innovation, customer experience, price-performance |
| **Rivalry** | Clear positioning, niche markets, strategic partnerships, operational excellence |

# Startup Metrics: 12 Important KPIs to Track in 2025

**Source:** [Gilion – Startup Metrics](https://www.gilion.com/basics/startup-metrics)  
**Topic:** Key performance indicators for startups

---

## Summary

Tracking the right startup metrics gives a clear picture of how product and marketing perform. Gilion outlines 12 key metrics to monitor, with guidance on selecting actionable KPIs aligned with business goals.

---

## 12 Key Metrics

| Metric | What it measures |
|--------|------------------|
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | Money spent to acquire new customers; marketing/sales efficiency |
| **Churn Rate** | % customers who cancel or stop using within a period; critical for recurring-revenue businesses |
| **Average Order Size** | Average spend per purchase; revenue health per customer |
| **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** | Expected monthly revenue from customers; growth and prediction |
| **ARR (Annual Run Rate)** | Expected annual revenue; growth and prediction |
| **Cash Runway** | Time to achieve profitability before running out of money |
| **Burn Rate** | Rate of spending; path to profitability |
| **K-factor** | Organic growth by word of mouth; virality |
| **Gross Sales** | Total revenue generated |
| **MAU (Monthly Active Users)** | Users who use product/service monthly |
| **NPS / Product-Market Fit** | Likelihood customers recommend; willingness to pay |
| **LTV/CAC Ratio** | Lifetime value vs acquisition cost; sustainability of sales/marketing |

---

## LTV/CAC Ratio

> For any professional startup founder, the LTV/CAC ratio is crucial to determine sustainability. A high ratio indicates efficient customer acquisition; a low ratio is a warning sign of unsustainable practices.

---

## Using KPIs for Strategic Decisions

- **Start with core values** – Identify what the company wants to achieve; use to guide KPI selection
- **Ensure KPIs are actionable** – Must be changeable through specific actions (e.g. customer satisfaction vs total revenue)
- **Align with strategy** – Each KPI should move the company toward its goals

---

## Picking Main Metrics

- Focus on a few key metrics relevant to your business
- Track over time to see performance and make adjustments
- Avoid tracking too many KPIs; too much data is overwhelming

---

## Traps to Avoid

- **Short-term gains vs long-term growth** – Don't sacrifice product or customer service for quick revenue
- **Vanity metrics** – Avoid metrics that feel good but don't drive decisions (e.g. raw website visitors)
- **Metrics as sole basis** – Use with strategy and judgment, not in isolation

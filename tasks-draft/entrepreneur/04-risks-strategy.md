## Strategy Frameworks

- **PESTEL** – Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal
- **VRIO** – Value, Rarity, Imitability, Organization
- **Porter's Five Forces** – Industry rivalry, new entrants, substitutes, buyers, suppliers

## How to Manage Risk in a New Business

1. **Recognize the Risks** – List financial, operational, and strategic threats.
2. **Measure Their Effect** – Prioritize through structured analysis and data.
3. **Respond with Strategy** – Develop plans that minimize disruption.
4. **Review and Adjust** – Evaluate performance and update mitigation plans.
5. **Align for Growth** – Connect risk management to overall strategy.

> Risk is not the enemy of progress; it is the test of it.

## FAQ

**Are the strategy tools customizable?**  
Yes. PESTEL, VRIO, and Porter’s Five Forces can be adjusted to industry, business model, and stage of growth.

**Can I integrate risk assessments into my business plan?**  
Yes. Insights and outputs can be exported into strategic or financial planning documents.

**Do I need consulting or legal expertise?**  
No. PrometAI guides you through structured tools without external consultants.

**Are these frameworks suitable for small startups?**  
Yes. They are flexible and simple to apply for early-stage startups.

**Why assess risks so early?**  
Early identification helps avoid costly surprises, align decisions with goals, and design resilient strategies.

**What is business risk?**  
Any factor that can prevent achieving your goals: financial uncertainty, operational challenges, market fluctuations, regulation, competition. Understanding them early protects performance and sustains growth.

## Summary

Business development is the intention to grow turned into strategy. It is not chasing quick wins but understanding where opportunities exist, how to reach them, and what structures will carry the business forward with sustainability. In 2026, leaders need carefully crafted BD strategies that respond to new opportunities while addressing ongoing challenges.

---

## What Is Business Development

- **BD is intention turned into strategy** – Understanding where opportunities exist, how to reach them, and what structures will carry the business forward.
- **Value:** Increase market share, boost revenue/profitability, maintain competitiveness, diversify risks.
- **Four stages:** Opportunity Analysis → Direction Setting → Execution → Monitoring & Adjustment.

---

## BD vs Business Continuity vs Business Contingency

| Criteria | Business Development | Business Continuity Plan | Business Contingency Plan |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Goal** | Drive growth, expand markets | Ensure operations continue during disruptions | Provide alternative actions if primary plan fails |
| **Orientation** | Long-term, proactive | Short–mid-term, reactive to crises | Short–mid-term, reactive to plan failure |
| **Focus** | Sales, partnerships, market expansion | Operations, infrastructure | Specific projects, strategic initiatives |
| **Trigger** | Strategic planning, opportunity spotting | Disruptions (natural disasters, outages) | Failure/blockage of main plan |

**In short:** BD = how to grow | BCP = how not to stop | Contingency = what to do if things go wrong.

## Growth Pathways – Practical Guideline

**1. Where to Grow (Choose Your Path)**

| Path | Action | Watch For | Measure |
|------|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Market Penetration** | Audit segments, loyalty programs, competitive analysis | Price wars, saturation | Market share %, retention |
| **Market Development** | Entry research, pilot projects | Cultural barriers, regulation | Revenue from new markets |
| **Product Development** | Customer feedback, PMF tests | Overstretching R&D | Adoption, upsell revenue |
| **Diversification** | Validate feasibility, differentiate | Brand credibility gaps | ROI, risk-adjusted returns |
| **M&A** | Acquisition thesis, shortlist targets | Integration, cultural mismatch | Time-to-synergy, deal ROI |
| **Strategic Partnerships** | Map partners, define value, structured agreements | Misaligned goals | Partner-driven revenue |

**2. How to Execute** – Channel strategy, partnership lifecycle, pricing/packaging, GTM architecture, land-and-expand, PLG.

**3. How to Measure** – KPI frameworks (pipeline 3–4× target, win rates, CAC, LTV, NRR), AI/automation, global readiness.

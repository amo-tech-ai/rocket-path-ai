# Porter's Five Forces

**Source:** [PrometAI Blueprint – Porter's Five Forces](https://prometai.app/blueprint/porters-five-forces)  
**Topic:** Competitive landscape and industry dynamics

---

## Summary

**Porter's Five Forces** provides a view of the competitive landscape. Analyzing these forces helps businesses understand factors influencing their market, develop strategies to enhance competitive position, and address threats. When combined with DCF analysis, it helps determine investment value by considering competitive dynamics.

---

## The Five Forces

| Force | What it assesses |
|-------|------------------|
| **1. Competitive Rivalry** | Number and capability of competitors; intensity of competition; price wars, marketing, product improvement |
| **2. Supplier Power** | Influence of suppliers on price and quality; few suppliers or unique products = higher power |
| **3. Buyer Power** | Customer ability to drive prices down, demand quality; alternatives and volume increase power |
| **4. Threat of Substitution** | Likelihood of customers finding alternatives; compels continuous innovation |
| **5. Threat of New Entry** | Ease of new entrants; barriers (capital, regulation) protect incumbents |

---

## How to Use Porter's Five Forces

1. **Threat of New Entrants** – Evaluate barriers (capital, tech); how easily can new competitors enter?
2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** – Identify key suppliers; assess influence on costs and supply chain
3. **Bargaining Power of Customers** – Understand ability to drive prices down; analyze volume and alternatives
4. **Threat of Substitutes** – Identify substitutes; assess switching ease
5. **Competitive Rivalry** – Examine number and strength of competitors; evaluate intensity

Use insights to develop strategic plans: innovation, customer service, strategic partnerships.

## FAQ

**What are the 5 forces of Porter?** Threat of New Entrants, Supplier Power, Customer Power, Threat of Substitutes, Competitive Rivalry.

**What does Porter's Five Forces determine?** Competitive intensity and attractiveness of an industry.

**Porter's Five Forces vs SWOT?** Porter focuses on industry structure; SWOT evaluates internal strengths/weaknesses and external opportunities/threats.

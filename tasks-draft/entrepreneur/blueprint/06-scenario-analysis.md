# Scenario Analysis

**Source:** [PrometAI Blueprint – Scenario Analysis](https://prometai.app/blueprint/scenario-analysis)  
**Topic:** Assessing potential future outcomes for decision-making

---

## Summary

**Scenario analysis** is a structured method of predicting future events by considering different potential developments. It constructs hypothetical scenarios—best-case, worst-case, baseline—and evaluates their impact on business, investment, or market. Valuable in volatile markets (recession, geopolitical instability, regulatory changes).

---

## Process

1. **Identify key variables** – Critical factors influencing financial or business environment
2. **Develop multiple scenarios** – Best-case, worst-case, base-case
3. **Analyze impact** – Influence on financial performance, market stability, investment
4. **Evaluate risks and opportunities** – Determine hazards and advantages in each scenario
5. **Implement strategic adjustments** – Develop contingency plans from insights

---

## Example: Manufacturing Expansion

- **Best-case:** Economic expansion drives demand; revenue growth
- **Worst-case:** Recession; demand drops; financial losses
- **Base-case:** Moderate growth; stable economy

Develop strategies to capitalize on best-case, mitigate worst-case, maintain stability in base-case.

---

## Benefits

- **Improves strategic decision-making** – e.g. product launch: market demand, competitive response, supply chain
- **Enhances risk assessment** – e.g. publicly traded company: market fluctuations, shareholder alignment
- **Strengthens financial stability** – e.g. manufacturing: raw material cost fluctuations, supplier contracts
- **Aids investment evaluation** – e.g. portfolio: interest rates, inflation, geopolitical impact
- **Supports corporate governance** – Align objectives with potential risks

---

## Scenario vs Sensitivity Analysis

| Scenario Analysis | Sensitivity Analysis |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Multiple interdependent factors | Single variable; others constant |
| Various possible future outcomes | Effect of one variable change |
| Best/worst/base scenarios | e.g. 1% interest rate change on valuation |
| Strategic planning, risk management | Finance, isolated variable impact |

Both complement each other; scenario explores breadth, sensitivity isolates specific variables.

---

## FAQ

**How to do scenario analysis?** Identify key variables → develop scenarios → analyze impact → refine strategies to mitigate risks.

**In strategic management?** Plan for future market conditions; identify hazards and opportunities; prepare for multiple outcomes.

**In risk management?** Evaluate risk scenarios to devise mitigation strategies.
